01
Principles
Zazig puts an autonomous executive team in the founder's hands. The design language reflects that relationship: strategic clarity, warm authority, and the quiet confidence of a system that runs while you sleep.
1
Strategic Clarity
Every pixel serves the founder's decision-making. Information density without noise. Show what matters, hide what doesn't.
2
Warm Authority
Not cold. Not casual. The tone of a trusted advisor briefing you over morning coffee. Editorial gravitas with human warmth.
3
Alive, Not Animated
Motion is purposeful. The dispatch dot breathes because the system is alive. Things move because state changed, not for decoration.
4
Grounded Materials
Colours named after physical things — paper, ink, stone, ember. The interface should feel like it could exist in the real world.
5
One Accent, Used Well
Ember is the single strong colour. It marks decisions, actions, and the live pulse of the system. Restraint over rainbow.
6
Progressive Disclosure
Show the headline first. Let the founder drill in if they want the detail. Default view is the executive summary, not the spreadsheet.
02
Colour
Named after materials, not numbers. Each colour has a physical-world analogue, grounding the interface in something tangible. The palette is warm: ivory backgrounds, near-black ink, and a single ember accent that marks everything that demands attention.

03
Typography
Two fonts. Plus Jakarta Sans does everything — weight and size create all the hierarchy. Heavy (800) for structural headings. Regular (400) at large sizes for personal, conversational moments. Bold (700) for card titles. JetBrains Mono for data and code. That's it.
